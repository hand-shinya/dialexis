Dialexis (διάλεξις, "discourse, reasoned examination") is a Reflexive Philosophy Infrastructure. To anyone with a philosophical question — from schoolchildren to specialists — it offers two things:

  1. A lens: a keyless, unified window onto the world's living free scholarly foundations (Wikidata, OpenAlex, Crossref, OpenCitations, Wikipedia/Wikisource, Project Gutenberg), every item stamped with its source and retrieval time.
  2. A research desk: a research-process graph whose first-class citizens are questions, claims, evidence, counterclaims, uncertainties and decisions — recording not the results of knowledge but how an inquiry actually proceeded, confidence-classified and exportable with provenance.

AI here is not an answer machine but an instrument for hypothesis generation, counterargument generation and level-adjusted explanation. Every AI-touched output is labeled, and remains "unverified" until a human checks the sources.

The full intellectual genealogy, design axioms and rebuild instructions are public: GENESIS.md (the constitution)
